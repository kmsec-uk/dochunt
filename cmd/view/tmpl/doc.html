☝ What does "revision" mean?

Throughout the lifetime of a document, it may undergo several revisions by editors. A larger revision number means more edits to a document.

Observers may see different revisions of the same document when visiting it over time, so we can use the revision as a chronological marker, which helps inform us of the document's lifecycle.

Note: imported documents (i.e. .docx) typically come with a standard revision of 1.
